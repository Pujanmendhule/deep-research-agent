## Summary
Quantum computers can solve cryptography problems by using quantum algorithms that can efficiently factor large numbers and solve discrete logarithms, which are the basis for many current encryption methods. However, this also means that quantum computers can potentially break many of today's widely used cryptographic systems. To address this threat, researchers are working on developing post-quantum cryptography algorithms that are secure against attacks by quantum computers. These algorithms include lattice-based cryptography, code-based cryptography, and multivariate cryptography, among others. The US government has established guidelines for the development and implementation of post-quantum cryptography, and many organizations are already working on transitioning to post-quantum cryptography systems.
